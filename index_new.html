Methodologies and Technical Experience

  • Molecular modeling and Structural bioinformatics – 5 Years

    • I have used Classical MD simulation approaches (all-atom), enhanced sampling techniques (steered MD, meta-dynamics), and druggability simulations to identify potential druggable and ligandable sites on targets of interest using DruGUI (NAMD). Additionally, I have experience in protein structure prediction (homology modeling), Alpha-Fold, and protein sequence analysis to understand protein-protein, protein-ligand, and protein-membrane systems and experienced on structural biology databases (PDB, UniProt, SCOP, Pfam).

  • Computer-aided-drug-design (CADD) – 8 Years

    • I have utilized Structure-Based Drug Design techniques, such as molecular docking, along with Ligand-Based Drug Design approaches, including pharmacophore modeling, QSAR, drug similarity searches, and virtual screening. Additionally, I have been understanding the Pharmacokinetic and Pharmacodynamic properties and Structure-Activity Relationships (SAR) and ADMET underlying concepts in Medicinal Chemistry for various drug compounds.

  • ML/AI for Structural bioinformatics and Drug discovery – 3 Years

    • I have gained experience with Deep Neural Networks (DNN), Recurrent Neural Networks (RNN), Graph-based Neural Networks, Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN), and machine learning models like Logistic Regression, Linear Regression, Support Vector Machines (SVM), K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN), Random Forest, Naïve Bayes, XGBoost, and Decision Trees. I have applied these techniques to supervised and unsupervised learning tasks, including hotspot residue prediction in the 15LOX-1/PEBP1 protein complex, virtual screening, drug repurposing, and QSAR analysis.
